Of the 2541 articles published that reference a "Taylor" in The Gaurdian in 2017, 264 are about Taylor Swift and 171 are about Taylor Ham. That means that these topics account for 10.39% and 6.73% of articles with Taylor mentions, but leaves 82.88% of total Taylor coverage yet unknown.

Methodology:

For the purposes of this analysis, all articles from The Gaurdian that contained the word "Taylor" in conjunction with "Swift" were assumed to have a reference to Taylor Swift, a popular musician, and those that contained "Taylor" in conjunction with "Ham" were assumed to be in reference to Taylor Ham, a popular lunch meat. It is, of course, possible that an article could mention a combination of those words without actualy refering to the targeted topics. For example, characterization of American track and field athlete Christian Taylor as "swift" or a recipe for Ham and Split Pea Soup by Miami's six-grade chef, Taylor Moxey, would bias results. A more robust analysis of categorical Taylor preferences in The Gaurdian requires additional research about the yet unidentified Taylor references.
